Building a portfolio is not just about showing work. It is about making a strong first impression, loading quickly, and staying easy to maintain as projects evolve.

## Performance matters

Recruiters and clients often skim dozens of sites in a short session. If a portfolio feels slow or clunky, the work inside it gets less attention.

Next.js gives you:

- Server-rendered pages for strong SEO
- Static generation for blog posts
- Image and font optimization out of the box

## Content should be easy to update

A portfolio that is painful to update quickly becomes outdated. Local MDX posts strike a nice balance:

1. Content lives in the repository
2. Posts are versioned with the site
3. You can replace dummy copy without touching layout code

## Small details compound

Typography, spacing, motion, and accessibility all signal craft. A polished portfolio does not need heavy animation. Subtle reveals, clear hierarchy, and readable copy go a long way.

If you are starting from scratch, ship a clean one-page site first. Add blog posts and deeper case studies as your work grows.
